Cheese Pizza

Description

Delight in the simplicity of a homemade cheese pizza! Start with a classic dough base, topped with a flavorful tomato sauce, and generously covered with melted cheese. Whether baked in the oven or cooked on the grill, this cheese pizza promises a satisfying meal for any occasion. Enjoy the comfort of gooey cheese and crispy crust in every bite, making it a perfect choice for a cozy night at home or a casual get-together with friends. Dive into the deliciousness of homemade pizza with this timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • Dough

    • 1/4 cups warm water
    • 1 tsp active dry yeast
    • 1 cup cold water
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 3 cups bread flour
    • 1 tbsp olive oil

  • Toppings

    • 6 oz mozzarella cheese (sliced)
    • 1/2 tsp dried oregano
    • 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
    • 1/2 cup canned crushed tomatoes (no salt)
    • 6 leaves fresh basil

Steps

  1. Dough

    1. Pour warm water into a large bowl, and sprinkle yeast ontop and rest for 5 minutes. Stir in some cold water and salt, as well as flour (1 cup at a time) until fully mixed.

    2. Spread flour on a surface, and knead dough until smooth (around 10 minutes).

    3. Cut the dough in half to form two dough balls and coat with olive oil. Put in refrigerator for at least 16 hours in a sealed container to allow dough to rise.

    4. Whenever you are going to make the pizza, remove the dough from the fridge an hour before using.

  2. Assembly

    1. Preheat the oven with a pizza stone to about 550F

    2. Prepare one dough ball by dusting with flour and streach to about 14 inches in diameter. Place on a cooking sheet or pizza peel.

    3. Put half of the mozzarella on the dough and sprinle oregano, and pepper on top. Add the tomatoes and drizzle some olive oil over it all.

    4. Once assembled, place the pizza on the stone inside the preheated oven for about 4-6 minutes.

    5. Remove pizza from oven after crust is golden brown, sprinkle the basil and serve!

    6. These steps can then be repeated again for the second dough ball.
